# Cardinal Architecture Specification — Volume III: Domains of Reality

Where Volumes I and II defined why Cardinal exists and how the engine is organized, Volume III
answers *what exists*: every major simulation domain, its responsibilities, boundaries,
interactions, and architectural contracts. The emphasis is on ownership and relationships,
not algorithms.

## Chapters

1. [Physical Reality](01_Physical_Reality.md) — space, topology, regions, containment, materials, environment
2. [Living Systems](02_Living_Systems.md) — organisms, needs, metabolism, lifecycle, inheritance
3. [Resources](03_Resources.md) — deposits, stocks, scarcity, regeneration, depletion
4. [Economy](04_Economy.md) — holdings, exchange, markets, production, money
5. [Society](05_Society.md) — kinship, households, settlements, roles, reputation
6. [Culture](06_Culture.md) — values, norms, customs, language, lore, transmission
7. [Knowledge](07_Knowledge.md) — techniques, skill, teaching, discovery, records, loss
8. [Conflict](08_Conflict.md) — grievances, hostility, engagements, morale, terms
9. [Institutions](09_Institutions.md) — offices, laws, jurisdiction, legitimacy, succession
10. [Ecology](10_Ecology.md) — populations, food webs, carrying capacity, migration, succession
11. [Emergence](11_Emergence.md) — how phenomena no domain owns arise, and the discipline that protects them
12. [Cross-Domain Interaction](12_Cross_Domain_Interaction.md) — the ownership matrix, effect chains, and composition law

Appendix A: [The Ownership Matrix](APPENDIX_A_Ownership_Matrix.md) — the complete
fact-ownership table and the twelve settled boundary rulings, recorded as normative
precedent.

Each chapter defines the domain's purpose, responsibilities, non-responsibilities, canonical
concepts, domain interactions, common queries, architectural contracts, engineering
invariants, anti-patterns, and future evolution.

The construction roadmap for Volumes III–V lives at [../ROADMAP.md](../ROADMAP.md).
