# domains/economy/

**Charter:** Vol. III Ch. 4 — Economy. Price is a fact discovered by exchange, never a
number assigned to a thing. No one sets prices (Ch. 4, Designer Note).

**Owns:** holdings; transfers (the atomic, conservative operation); exchanges and their
cleared ratios (prices as recorded events); money and its circulation; production
recipes' execution; markets and their local price memory; standing obligations.

**Never owns:** the goods' physical existence (Physical/Resources), legitimacy of
ownership (Society/Institutions), desire (Living Systems via decisions), transport
itself (Physical), enforcement (Institutions), global statistics — derived, never
stored (§4.3).

**Key contracts:** goods and money conserved through every transfer; prices are local
to markets; money issuance is an explicit institutional act (Ruling 10); production
consumes real held inputs; distance imposes real cost on all movement of goods (§4.8).

**Appendix A:** Ruling 10 (Economy circulates, Institutions authorize issuance).
