# domains/culture/

**Charter:** Vol. III Ch. 6 — Culture. What a community knows without ever having been
told: shared meaning, transmitted generationally, capable of reshaping the material
world through behavior (the taboo forest, Ch. 6 Designer Note).

**Owns:** cultures as entities; values; norms (expectation only — never enforcement);
customs; languages and intelligibility; lore (which may be false); cultural membership,
transmission, and drift.

**Never owns:** individual belief (information layer), enforcement (Society's
reputation / Institutions' sanction — Ruling 3), the groups themselves (Society),
truth (lore never edits the chronicle — Ruling 6), formal doctrine (Institutions) (§6.3).

**Key contracts:** all cultural content is world-package data; language boundaries are
real barriers to information flow; transmission is generational and channel-based —
no instant assimilation; a culture with no living carriers is dead, recorded, not
deleted (§6.8).

**Appendix A:** Ruling 3 (norm vs. law), Ruling 6 (lore never edits history).
