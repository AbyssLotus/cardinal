# domains/knowledge/

**Charter:** Vol. III Ch. 7 — Knowledge. Capability is carried, taught, and mortal.
Every skill is one unbroken chain of teachers away from being lost (the last smith of
Milbrook, Ch. 7 Designer Note).

**Owns:** techniques and prerequisite graphs (definitions as package data); knowing and
proficiency (per mind); learning, teaching, and practice; discovery (singular, located,
seeded — never scheduled); records' inscribed content; loss.

**Never owns:** situational information ("the bridge is out" — Ruling 8: state vs.
capability), the performance itself (Living/Physical), recipes' material truth
(Economy/Resources data), institutions of learning (Institutions), the physical book
(Physical — Ruling 11) (§7.3).

**Key contracts:** no knowledge without a carrier — no global technology state, ever;
transmission is explicit, costly, and gated by language; skill decays without practice;
a technique with no carriers and no accessible records is extinct (§7.8). No research
bars (§7.9).

**Appendix A:** Ruling 8 (knowledge vs. information), Ruling 11 (content vs. medium).
